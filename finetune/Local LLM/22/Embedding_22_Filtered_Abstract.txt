Flow-chemistry process development: A novel continuous flow polymerization system was designed for the synthesis of branched polymers using a microreactor with a miniemulsion system. The reaction type involved controlled radical polymerization (CRP) with azobisisobutyronitrile (AIBN) as initiator, styrene and divinylbenzene as key reactants, toluene as solvent, and potassium persulfate as catalyst. The products were polystyrene-poly(divinylbenzene)-polystyrene block copolymers with varying branching degrees. The microreactor had a 100 µm ID and was made of stainless steel, with a flow rate of 2 mL/min and residence time of 36 min at room temperature (25°C) and atmospheric pressure. The critical condition for the polymerization process was the concentration of AIBN, which significantly affected the branching degree. The outcomes showed that the conversion and yield were high, while the selectivity towards the desired block copolymers with higher branching degrees improved in the microreactor compared to the batch processing due to reduced mass transport limitations and more homogeneous temperature control.

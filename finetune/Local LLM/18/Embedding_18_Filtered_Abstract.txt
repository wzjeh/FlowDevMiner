Flow-chemistry process development: A continuous flow microreactor system was used for the synthesis of 5-nitro salicylic acid (5NSA) from sodium acetate (SA) and acetic anhydride (AcOH). The reactor had a coiled tube with an inner diameter of 1.38 mm, and the flow rate was controlled by a syringe pump. The residence time in the microreactor ranged from 2 to 60 minutes. The temperature was maintained at 20°C, and the pressure was atmospheric. The key reactants were SA:AcOH with a mole ratio of 1:5, and HNO3 as a catalyst. The product was 5NSA, and the reactor type was a microreactor system. The critical conditions included flow rates ranging from 0.2 to 4 mL/min, residence times between 2-60 minutes, temperature at 20°C, and atmospheric pressure. Outcomes were monitored by measuring the conversion and yield of 5NSA.
